About

From physics to clinically grounded AI.

I graduated in physics at the University of Genova, Italy, completed my master's there in physics, and earned my PhD in computer science in May 2020.

During my PhD, I was a visiting PhD student at MIT. I later joined MIT and Fujitsu Research of America for postdoctoral research focused on machine learning, systematic generalization, and robust AI.

Today I work at the intersection of machine learning, healthcare, medical education, and global health, with projects spanning pediatric endocrinology, clinical AI evaluation, and interoperable care workflows in Jamaica and the Galapagos Islands. I am also part of the inaugural NSF NAIRR AI Education Fellows cohort.
